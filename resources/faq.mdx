## Getting started

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is Convo?">
    Convo is an AI-moderated focus group platform. You write a discussion guide, recruit participants, and our AI moderator runs live multi-participant video sessions for you. After sessions finish, Convo analyzes everything and gives you themes, quotes, video clips, and a full summary.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How is this different from a survey tool?">
    Surveys give you structured, quantitative data. Convo gives you qualitative depth — real conversations where participants explain their thinking, react to each other, and go off-script. The AI moderator asks follow-up questions based on what people actually say, so you get the "why" behind responses, not just the "what."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need research experience to use Convo?">
    No. The AI Study Builder generates a discussion guide, screener, and settings from a plain-language description of what you want to learn. You can edit everything afterward, but you don't need to start from scratch.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long does it take to set up a study?">
    A few minutes with the AI Study Builder. Describe your research goals, review the generated guide, tweak anything you want, and launch. Manual setup takes longer but gives you full control over every detail.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Study design

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's in a discussion guide?">
    A discussion guide is organized into **moments** — the individual topics or questions you want covered. Each moment has a topic, goal, priority level, optional follow-up probes, and optional stimulus materials (images, videos, URLs). The AI moderator works through moments in order but adapts based on how the conversation goes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I upload an existing discussion guide?">
    Yes. Upload a PDF or text file and Convo converts it into the structured format automatically. You can then edit it in the platform.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What are moment priorities?">
    Each moment can be Low, Medium, or High priority. If a session runs long, the AI moderator spends less time on lower-priority moments so the important ones get covered.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What formats work for stimuli?">
    Images (PNG, JPG, GIF), videos, and URLs. Attach them to specific moments in your guide and the AI moderator shows them automatically during the session.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I edit my study after launching?">
    Some things, yes — like adding new recruitment rounds. But the screener can't be changed once participants have started responding, since that would affect data consistency. Finalize your screener and guide before going live.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What screener question types are available?">
    Multiple choice (single or multi-select), open text, number (with optional range), and yes/no. You can mark specific answers as disqualifying to screen people out automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The AI moderator

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How does the AI moderator work?">
    It speaks to participants in natural, conversational language. It follows your discussion guide, asks follow-up questions based on what people actually say, manages turn-taking, shows stimulus materials, and wraps up the session. You don't need to be there.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does the AI moderator ask follow-up questions?">
    Yes. It generates follow-ups based on what participants say, not just the scripted probes. If someone says something interesting or vague, it digs deeper. You can also add specific probes to any moment in your guide.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does it handle off-topic responses?">
    It acknowledges the comment and steers the conversation back to the current topic. If the tangent is relevant to a later moment, it may note it and come back to it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does it handle turn-taking in groups?">
    The AI moderator uses speaker identification to know who's talking. It can direct questions to specific people, invite quieter participants to share, handle overlapping speech, and reference what someone said earlier.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the Director Agent?">
    A secondary AI that runs behind the scenes during sessions. It tracks remaining time, tells the AI moderator when to move on or go deeper, ensures high-priority moments get covered, flags quality issues, and can reorder moments based on how the conversation is going. You don't interact with it directly.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Participants and recruitment

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I recruit participants?">
    Two ways: use Convo's built-in recruitment panels (general population is available directly) or share a direct link with your own participant list. Either way, participants go through your screener and a video/audio check before joining.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How many participants can be in a session?">
    You set the group size when creating your study. Smaller groups (3–5 people) tend to get richer discussion. Larger groups cover more perspectives per session.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use Convo for 1:1 interviews?">
    Yes. Set the group size to 1 and the AI moderator runs a one-on-one interview. Analysis works the same way — you still get themes and clips across all your sessions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I get B2B or professional recruitment?">
    General population panels are available directly in Convo. For professional audiences (decision-makers, developers, healthcare workers, etc.), [email us](mailto:support@useconvo.ai) and we'll set up a recruitment plan.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I bring my own participants?">
    Yes. Copy your study's unique participant link from the Recruitment tab and share it via email, Slack, social media — wherever. Participants click the link, fill out your screener, pass a video/audio check, and get scheduled into a session.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the participant funnel?">
    Every participant goes through the same stages: New → Screener → Video check → Qualified → Signed up → Joined. You can watch the funnel in real time from the Recruitment tab.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I recruit in rounds?">
    Yes. Each round specifies how many additional participants you need. Useful for running a small pilot first, adding diversity to the sample, or running longitudinal studies over multiple weeks.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if a participant has technical issues?">
    Everyone does a video and audio check before joining, which catches most issues. If something comes up during a session, the Director Agent monitors quality and the AI moderator adjusts accordingly.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Sessions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I observe sessions in real time?">
    Right now, the AI moderator runs sessions and records them automatically. You can review recordings and transcripts as soon as a session ends. Real-time observation is on our roadmap.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long are sessions?">
    You choose: 30, 45, or 60 minutes per session.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What do participants see during a session?">
    A video call interface. They see the AI moderator's avatar, other participants' video feeds, and any stimulus materials. They talk naturally, like a regular video call.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What are the session stages?">
    Every session goes through: Waiting room → Intro → Discussion → Conclusion → Ended. The AI moderator manages each stage automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Analysis and results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How long does analysis take?">
    Usually a few minutes, depending on how many sessions and how much discussion there was. You'll get notified when results are ready.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I run analysis after each session, or do I have to wait?">
    Either. You can run analysis after each session or wait until all sessions are done. Analyzing everything together gives you better cross-session insights.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What results do I get?">
    Five types:
    - **Summary** — findings, decisions, agreements, debates, and metrics
    - **Deep Dive & Responses** — moment-by-moment analysis and per-participant views
    - **Themes** — patterns across all sessions with supporting quotes
    - **Video Clips** — auto-generated clips of key moments
    - **Explore** — a chat interface to ask questions about your data
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's in the Summary?">
    Key metrics (participant count, session count, consensus rate), decision cards with verdicts and confidence levels, key findings, agreements, debates (with Side A and Side B positions), emergent insights, and auto-generated charts.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do themes work?">
    Convo groups related statements from across all transcripts into themes. Each theme shows a name, description, participant count, connected objectives, connected moments, and direct quotes attributed to specific participants. Themes can span multiple topics in your guide.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What can I do with Explore?">
    Ask questions in plain language — like "What did participants think about pricing?" or "Compare how power users and new users responded." Explore searches your transcripts, cites specific participants and clips, and can generate charts. Each answer includes a confidence level.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How are video clips generated?">
    Convo automatically identifies key moments from recordings — strong opinions, emotional reactions, debates, clear articulations of findings — and turns them into short, shareable clips. You can browse and filter by topic, participant, or theme.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I filter results?">
    Yes. Themes can be filtered by objective, moment, or participant. Deep Dive organizes by moment, Responses organizes by participant. Clips can be filtered by topic, participant, or theme.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data, security, and privacy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How is my data stored?">
    All data is encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest. Study data lives in managed databases with access controls. Session recordings are in secure cloud storage with time-limited access URLs.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is my data used to train AI models?">
    No. Data sent to third-party services (transcription, text-to-speech, video processing, AI analysis) is used only for processing your sessions. It's not used to train third-party models.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What about GDPR?">
    Participants consent during signup before any data is collected. They're told sessions will be recorded. You can request deletion of participant data by contacting us. Processing is covered by our data processing agreement. Need a DPA? [Email us](mailto:support@useconvo.ai).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I delete participant data?">
    Yes. [Email us](mailto:support@useconvo.ai) to request deletion. Study data stays as long as your account is active. Archived studies remain accessible but aren't actively processed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can see my study data?">
    Participant data is scoped to your study and only accessible to users with permission. It's not shared across studies or with other customers.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
