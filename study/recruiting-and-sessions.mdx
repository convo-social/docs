Once your study is live, you need participants. After they join, the AI moderator takes over.

## Recruiting participants

Two ways to get people into your study:

### Our panels

General population participants are available directly in Convo. Set your target, specify how many people you need, and launch. Participants are sourced, screened, and scheduled automatically.

For professional or B2B recruitment (decision-makers, developers, healthcare workers), [email us](mailto:support@useconvo.ai).

### Bring your own

Have your own list? Copy your study's unique participant link from the Recruitment tab and share it via email, Slack, wherever. Participants click the link, fill out your screener, pass a video/audio check, and get scheduled.

### The participant funnel

Everyone goes through the same stages:

| Stage | What happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **New** | Accessed the study link |
| **Screener** | Filling out screener questions |
| **Video check** | Verifying camera and mic |
| **Qualified** | Passed screener + video check |
| **Signed up** | Confirmed and scheduled |
| **Joined** | Completed a session |

### Recruiting in rounds

You can recruit in batches. Start with a small pilot round (3–5 participants) to test your screener and guide before going full scale. Add more rounds as you need them.

## What happens during a session

<Frame>
  [VIDEO: Loom showing a live session in action — the AI moderator running a focus group with multiple participants. ~2 minutes.]
</Frame>

### The AI moderator

The AI moderator speaks naturally and runs each session following your guide. It:

- Welcomes participants and explains the format
- Works through your moments in order
- Asks follow-up questions based on what people actually say
- Handles turn-taking so everyone gets heard
- Shows stimulus when a moment calls for it
- Wraps up and thanks everyone

It adapts on the fly. If someone raises something relevant to a later topic, it can rearrange the order. If a high-priority moment needs more time, it spends less on lower-priority ones.

### The Director Agent

Behind the scenes, a secondary AI monitors each session:

- Tracks remaining time and pacing
- Makes sure high-priority moments get covered
- Flags audio or video quality issues
- Can reorder moments based on the conversation flow

You don't interact with it — it runs automatically.

### Multi-participant handling

The AI moderator uses speaker identification to know who's talking. It directs questions to specific people, invites quieter participants to share, handles overlapping speech, and references what someone said earlier.

### Session stages

| Stage | What happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **Waiting room** | Participants join and wait |
| **Intro** | AI moderator introduces itself and sets expectations |
| **Discussion** | The main conversation through your guide |
| **Conclusion** | Summary, final thoughts, thank-you |
| **Ended** | Recordings and transcripts saved |

## What participants see

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The participant view during a session — AI moderator avatar, other participants' video feeds, and stimulus display]
</Frame>

Before joining, every participant does a video and audio check — camera working, mic picking up clear audio, stable internet. If something fails, they fix it before they can join.

During the session, it feels like a video call. They see the AI moderator's avatar, other participants' video feeds, and any stimulus materials. They just talk naturally.

## Tips

- **Smaller groups get richer discussion.** 3–5 participants per session tends to work well. Larger groups cover more perspectives but each person talks less.
- **Run a pilot round first.** 3–5 participants to test your screener and guide. Fix issues before recruiting everyone.
- **You don't need to be there.** Sessions are recorded and transcribed automatically. Review them after.
