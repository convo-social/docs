Convo automatically identifies the most important moments from your sessions and creates video clips. It also generates a study trailer -- a highlight reel you can share with anyone.

## Video clips

After analysis, Convo produces a set of video clips pulled from your session recordings. Each clip captures a key moment: a strong opinion, an emotional reaction, a debate between participants, or a clear articulation of a finding.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The clips gallery showing a grid of auto-generated clips with thumbnails, participant names, and topic labels]
</Frame>

### Browsing clips

Clips are organized by the moment they relate to. You can browse all clips or filter by:

- Discussion topic or moment
- Participant
- Theme

Each clip shows a thumbnail, the participant who is speaking, and a short description of what the clip captures.

### Previewing clips

Click any clip to preview it inline. You will see the video alongside the transcript for that segment.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The clip player showing a video clip with the transcript displayed alongside it]
</Frame>

### Clip statuses

Clips go through processing stages:

- **Generating** -- The clip is being created from the raw session recording
- **Enhanced** -- The clip has been processed with improved audio and visual quality
- **Ready** -- The clip is available for viewing and sharing

---

## Study trailer

The study trailer is an auto-generated highlight reel that combines the best clips from your study into a single, shareable video.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The trailer preview showing the auto-generated highlight reel with clips from multiple participants]
</Frame>

### What the trailer includes

The trailer pulls together:

- Clips that represent your key findings
- A mix of participants to show breadth of perspectives
- Clips from your highest-priority moments

### Sharing the trailer

Click **Share** to get a public URL for your trailer. Anyone with the link can view it -- no Convo account required.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The share dialog showing the public trailer URL and copy button]
</Frame>

This makes trailers ideal for:

- **Stakeholder presentations** -- Share the link in a Slack message or email. Stakeholders can watch the highlights without sitting through full sessions.
- **Team alignment** -- Give your product, design, and engineering teams a visceral sense of what users said.
- **Executive buy-in** -- A 2-minute trailer is more convincing than a 20-page report.

<Tip>
  Include the trailer link in your research readout. Seeing real participants express real opinions is far more impactful than reading a summary.
</Tip>
