The Themes tab shows you patterns that Convo identified across all sessions. These are recurring ideas, sentiments, or behaviors that appeared in multiple participants' responses.

## What theme analysis is

After analyzing all transcripts, Convo groups related participant statements into themes. Each theme represents a distinct pattern -- something multiple participants said, felt, or demonstrated.

Themes go beyond individual moments. A single theme might draw evidence from multiple topics in your discussion guide, giving you a cross-cutting view of your data.

---

## Viewing themes

Each theme includes:

- **Theme name** -- A short, descriptive label
- **Description** -- What this theme represents and why it matters
- **Participant count** -- How many participants expressed this theme
- **Connected objectives** -- Which of your research objectives this theme relates to
- **Connected moments** -- Which discussion topics surfaced this theme
- **Supporting quotes** -- Direct quotes from participants, each attributed to a specific person

## Filtering themes

Use the filter panel to narrow down themes by:

- **Objective** -- Show only themes related to a specific research objective
- **Moment** -- Show only themes that emerged from a specific discussion topic
- **Participant** -- Show only themes that a specific participant contributed to

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The filter panel showing objective, moment, and participant filter options]
</Frame>

This is especially useful when you are writing up findings for a specific research question. Filter by your objective, and you will see only the relevant themes with their supporting evidence.

---

## Using themes for synthesis and reporting

Themes are one of the most useful outputs for creating research deliverables:

- **Research reports** -- Use themes as your main sections, with supporting quotes as evidence
- **Stakeholder presentations** -- Lead with the top themes and their participant counts to show the weight of evidence
- **Affinity mapping** -- Themes serve as pre-built affinity groups you can use directly or refine
- **Tracking over time** -- Compare themes across studies to see how user perspectives evolve

<Info>
  Themes are auto-generated but not set in stone. Use them as a starting point and combine or refine them as you build your narrative.
</Info>
