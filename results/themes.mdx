The Themes tab shows recurring patterns across your sessions — ideas, sentiments, or behaviors that came up with multiple participants.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The theme list showing all identified themes with participant counts and brief descriptions]

## How themes work

Convo groups related statements from across all transcripts into themes. Each theme is something multiple participants said, felt, or demonstrated.

Themes aren't limited to individual moments. A single theme might pull evidence from several topics in your guide, giving you a cross-cutting view.

---

## What each theme shows

- **Name** — a short label
- **Description** — what the theme is about
- **Participant count** — how many people expressed it
- **Connected objectives** — which research objectives it ties to
- **Connected moments** — which discussion topics it came from
- **Quotes** — direct quotes, each attributed to a specific participant

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: A single theme detail view showing the description, participant count, connected objectives, and supporting quotes with attribution]

---

## Filtering

Narrow down themes by:

- **Objective** — only themes related to a specific research question
- **Moment** — only themes from a specific discussion topic
- **Participant** — only themes a specific person contributed to

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The filter panel showing objective, moment, and participant filter options]
</Frame>

Helpful when you're writing up findings for a specific research question. Filter by objective and you'll see just the relevant themes with their evidence.

---

## Using themes in your work

- **Research reports** — use themes as your main sections, with quotes as evidence
- **Stakeholder presentations** — lead with top themes and participant counts
- **Affinity mapping** — themes work as pre-built affinity groups
- **Tracking over time** — compare themes across studies to see how perspectives shift

<Info>
  Themes are generated automatically, but they're a starting point. Combine or refine them as you build your narrative.
</Info>
