The Summary is the first place to go after analysis completes. It gives you the big picture: what you learned, what participants agreed on, where they disagreed, and what the AI recommends.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The full Summary view showing the overview section, metrics cards, and findings]
</Frame>

## Overview and metrics

At the top of the Summary, you will see key metrics for your study:

- **Participant count** -- Total number of participants across all sessions
- **Session count** -- How many sessions were completed
- **Consensus rate** -- Percentage of topics where participants broadly agreed
- **Debates** -- Number of topics where participants had opposing views

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The metrics cards row showing participant count, session count, consensus rate, and debates count]
</Frame>

## Decisions

For each objective in your study, Convo generates a **decision card** with:

- **Verdict** -- A clear, actionable conclusion (e.g., "Participants strongly prefer Option A")
- **Confidence** -- High, Moderate, or Low, based on the strength and consistency of the evidence
- **Evidence** -- The data points that support the verdict
- **Pulls** -- Factors that attracted participants toward a position
- **Pushes** -- Factors that pushed participants away from a position
- **Key quotes** -- Direct quotes from participants that illustrate the finding
- **Recommendations** -- AI-generated suggestions for next steps

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: A single decision card showing the verdict, confidence level, evidence, pulls/pushes, and quotes]
</Frame>

<Tip>
  Pay special attention to **Low confidence** decisions. These indicate topics where you may need more data or a follow-up study.
</Tip>

---

## Key findings

Below the decisions, you will find key findings that span across your objectives. Each finding includes:

- A clear statement of the finding
- The number of participants who expressed this view
- Supporting quotes

These are the headline insights you would put in a research report or stakeholder presentation.

---

## Agreements

Agreements are topics where participants reached consensus. Convo identifies these automatically by looking for patterns where most participants expressed similar views.

Each agreement shows:

- The consensus point
- How many participants aligned with this view
- Representative quotes

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The agreements section showing consensus points with participant counts and quotes]
</Frame>

---

## Debates

Debates are topics where participants had meaningfully different opinions. Each debate shows:

- **Side A** -- One position with supporting participants and quotes
- **Side B** -- The opposing position with its supporters and quotes

Debates are some of the most valuable outputs from focus groups. They highlight genuine tensions and trade-offs in how your audience thinks about a topic.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The debates section showing a contested topic with Side A and Side B positions]
</Frame>

---

## Emergent insights

Sometimes participants raise topics you did not plan for. Convo surfaces these as **emergent insights** -- unexpected findings that came up organically during sessions.

These can reveal blind spots in your research plan or surface opportunities you had not considered.
