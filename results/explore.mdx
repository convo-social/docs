Explore is a chat interface for your study data. Ask questions in plain language and it searches your transcripts, cites specific participants and clips, and generates charts.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The Explore chat interface showing a conversation with questions and AI-generated answers]
</Frame>

## What you can do

Explore has access to everything: transcripts, participant data, themes, analysis results. Some things to try:

- "What did participants think about the pricing model?"
- "How did younger participants respond differently from older ones?"
- "Show me quotes where participants mentioned competitor X"
- "Were there any topics where men and women disagreed?"
- "Create a chart showing sentiment breakdown by topic"

Answers link back to specific participants and clips so you can trace the source.

---

## Confidence indicators

Each answer comes with a confidence level:

- **High** — clear, consistent evidence from multiple participants
- **Moderate** — some evidence, but with caveats
- **Low** — limited evidence. Treat it as a lead to investigate, not a conclusion.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: An Explore answer showing the confidence indicator badge]
</Frame>

<Info>
  Low confidence? Try rephrasing or asking about something more specific.
</Info>

## Example questions

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Summary | "Summarize what participants said about onboarding" |
| Comparison | "Compare how power users and new users responded to the redesign" |
| Quote finding | "Find quotes about frustrations with the current checkout flow" |
| Pattern detection | "What unexpected topics came up that weren't in the guide?" |
| Specifics | "What did Participant 3 say about pricing?" |
| Charts | "Create a chart showing how many participants mentioned each competitor" |

---

## Charts

When you ask Explore to visualize something, it generates charts inline. These pull from actual transcript data, so the numbers are grounded in what people said.

  [IMAGE: An Explore-generated chart showing a data visualization created from study data]

---

## Conversations

You can have multiple conversations per study. Start a new one for each research question. Previous conversations are saved in the sidebar.

---

## Suggested questions

First time opening Explore? Convo suggests questions based on your objectives and results. Good starting point if you're not sure what to ask.

---

## File upload

Upload files to give Explore more context. For example, upload a competitor analysis doc and ask Explore to compare participant feedback against it.
