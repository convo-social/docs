Explore is Convo's AI-powered chat interface for your study data. Ask questions in plain language, and Explore searches your transcripts, cites specific participants and clips, and can even generate charts.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The Explore chat interface showing a conversation with questions and AI-generated answers]
</Frame>

## What Explore can do

Explore has access to everything from your study: transcripts, participant data, themes, and analysis results. You can ask it to:

- **Summarize** -- "What did participants think about the pricing model?"
- **Compare** -- "How did younger participants respond differently from older ones?"
- **Find quotes** -- "Show me quotes where participants mentioned competitor X"
- **Detect patterns** -- "Were there any topics where men and women disagreed?"
- **Generate charts** -- "Create a chart showing sentiment breakdown by topic"
- **Cite sources** -- Explore links answers back to specific participants and clips

---

## Confidence indicators

Each answer from Explore includes a confidence indicator. This tells you how strongly the evidence supports the answer:

- **High confidence** -- Clear, consistent evidence from multiple participants
- **Moderate confidence** -- Some supporting evidence but with caveats
- **Low confidence** -- Limited evidence; treat as a hypothesis to investigate further

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: An Explore answer showing the confidence indicator badge]
</Frame>

<Info>
  If Explore shows low confidence, try rephrasing your question or asking about a more specific aspect of the topic.
</Info>

## Example question types

Here are some effective ways to use Explore:

| Question type | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Summary | "Summarize what participants said about onboarding" |
| Comparison | "Compare how power users and new users responded to the redesign" |
| Quote finding | "Find quotes about frustrations with the current checkout flow" |
| Pattern detection | "What unexpected topics came up that were not in the discussion guide?" |
| Specifics | "What did Participant 3 say about pricing?" |
| Charts | "Create a chart showing how many participants mentioned each competitor" |

---

## Generated charts

When you ask Explore to visualize data, it generates charts inline in the conversation. These charts pull from the actual transcript data, so the numbers are grounded in what participants said.

## Managing conversations

You can have multiple conversations within Explore. This is useful when you are investigating different aspects of your study:

- Start a new conversation for each research question
- Previous conversations are saved and accessible from the sidebar
- Each conversation maintains its own context

---

## Suggested questions

When you first open Explore, Convo suggests questions based on your study objectives and analysis results. These are a good starting point if you are not sure what to ask.

---

## File upload

You can upload files to Explore to give it additional context. For example, upload a competitor analysis document and ask Explore to compare participant feedback against your competitive landscape.
