Once participants are recruited and scheduled, Convo handles everything. Peggy, the AI moderator, runs each session following your discussion guide while the Director Agent monitors quality behind the scenes.

## How AI moderation works

Peggy is Convo's AI moderator. She speaks to participants using natural, conversational language. During each session, Peggy:

- Welcomes participants and explains how the session works
- Works through your discussion guide moment by moment
- Asks follow-up questions and probes based on what participants say
- Manages turn-taking so every participant gets heard
- Presents stimulus materials when a moment calls for them
- Wraps up the session and thanks participants

Peggy adapts in real time. If a participant raises something relevant to a later topic, Peggy can adjust the order. If a high-priority moment needs more time, she will spend less time on lower-priority ones.

## Session stages

Every session moves through five stages:

| Stage | What happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **Waiting Room** | Participants join and wait for the session to begin. Peggy starts once enough participants are present. |
| **Intro** | Peggy introduces herself, explains the format, and sets expectations. |
| **Discussion** | The main session. Peggy works through your discussion guide, asking questions and facilitating conversation. |
| **Conclusion** | Peggy summarizes the discussion, asks for any final thoughts, and thanks participants. |
| **Ended** | The session is over. Recordings and transcripts are saved for analysis. |

---

## The Director Agent

Working behind the scenes, the Director Agent is a secondary AI that monitors each session and makes real-time decisions:

- **Time management** -- Tracks how much time is left and signals Peggy to move on or go deeper
- **Coverage monitoring** -- Ensures all high-priority moments get addressed
- **Quality checks** -- Flags if audio or video quality drops for any participant
- **Adaptive flow** -- Can reorder moments based on how the conversation unfolds

You do not interact with the Director Agent directly. It works automatically to ensure session quality.

---

## Multi-participant handling

Convo is built for group sessions. Peggy uses speaker identification to know who is talking and can:

- Direct questions to specific participants
- Invite quieter participants to share their perspective
- Manage overlapping speech
- Reference what a specific participant said earlier in the conversation

<Info>
  Peggy identifies speakers by their video and audio. Each participant's contributions are individually attributed in transcripts and analysis.
</Info>

---

## Stimulus presentation

If you attached stimulus materials to any moments in your discussion guide, Peggy presents them automatically when she reaches that moment. Participants see the stimulus on their screen while the discussion continues.

## Video and audio check

Before joining a session, every participant completes an AI-powered video and audio check. This ensures:

- Their camera is working and their face is visible
- Their microphone is picking up clear audio
- Their internet connection is stable enough for a video call

Participants who fail the check are prompted to fix the issue before they can join.

## What participants experience

From the participant's perspective, the flow is:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Join page">
    Participant clicks the session link and sees a branded join page with the session details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Device settings">
    Participant selects their camera and microphone, and completes the video/audio check.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Waiting room">
    Participant waits for the session to begin. They see how many others have joined.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The session">
    Peggy runs the session. Participants see Peggy's avatar, other participants' video feeds, and any stimulus materials. They speak naturally as they would in a video call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Session ends">
    Peggy wraps up and the session ends. Participants see a thank-you screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>
