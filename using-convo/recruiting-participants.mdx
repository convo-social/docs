Two ways to get participants into your study: our built-in panels or a direct link to your own people.

## Our panels

Convo has integrated recruitment panels, so you can find participants without leaving the platform.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The panel recruitment interface showing audience targeting options and participant count settings]
</Frame>

### General population

Available directly in Convo. Set your target audience, specify how many people you need, and launch. Participants are sourced, screened, and scheduled automatically.

### Professional and B2B

Need to reach decision-makers, developers, healthcare workers, or other specialized groups? [Contact our team](mailto:support@useconvo.ai) to discuss B2B recruitment.

<Info>
  Panel participants still go through your screener. The panel provides the initial pool; your screener narrows it down.
</Info>

## Bring your own participants

Have your own list? Share a direct link and let them sign up.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The direct link sharing interface showing the unique study URL and copy button]
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy your study link">
    Go to the Recruitment tab and copy the unique participant link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share it">
    Send the link via email, Slack, social media, wherever.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Participants sign up">
    They click the link, fill out your screener, pass a video and audio check, and get scheduled into a session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Participant funnel

Everyone goes through the same stages, regardless of how they were recruited:

| Stage | What happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **New** | Accessed the study link |
| **Screener** | Filling out screener questions |
| **Video check** | Verifying their camera and mic work |
| **Qualified** | Passed screener + video check |
| **Signed up** | Confirmed and scheduled for a session |
| **Joined** | Entered and completed a session |

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The recruitment dashboard showing participant counts at each funnel stage]
</Frame>

You can watch the funnel in real time from the Recruitment tab.

## Recruitment rounds

You can recruit in rounds. Each round specifies how many additional participants you need. Useful when:

- You want a small pilot before going full scale
- Early sessions show you need more diversity in the sample
- You're running a longitudinal study over multiple weeks

<Tip>
  Start with a small round (3-5 participants) to test your screener and guide before recruiting everyone.
</Tip>
