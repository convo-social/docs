Every Convo study has three parts: a **discussion guide** (the topics your AI moderator will cover), a **screener** (to qualify participants), and **interview settings** (duration, group size, and welcome screen). You can build everything using the AI Study Builder or the manual editor.

## AI Study Builder

The fastest way to create a study. Describe your research goals in plain language and Convo generates a complete study for you.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The AI Study Builder chat interface showing the step-by-step conversation flow]
</Frame>

The builder walks you through five steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe your research">
    Tell Convo what you want to learn. You can type a description or upload an existing discussion guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the discussion guide">
    Convo generates a discussion guide with topics, questions, and follow-up probes. Review and edit as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your screener">
    Convo suggests screener questions based on your research goals. Add, remove, or edit questions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure settings">
    Set the session duration, number of participants per session, and customize the welcome screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch">
    Review everything and activate your study. You are ready to recruit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can upload an existing discussion guide (PDF or text) and Convo will convert it into the structured format automatically.
</Tip>

## Discussion guide

The discussion guide is the script Peggy follows during sessions. It is organized into **moments** -- the individual topics or questions you want to cover.

### Moments

Each moment in your guide has:

- **Topic** -- The subject Peggy will discuss with participants
- **Goal** -- What you want to learn from this moment
- **Priority** -- Low, Medium, or High. High-priority moments get more time if sessions run long.
- **Probes** -- Follow-up questions Peggy can use to dig deeper
- **Stimulus** -- Optional images, videos, or other media to show participants during this moment

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: A single moment configuration showing topic, goal, priority, probes, and stimulus fields]
</Frame>

You can reorder moments by dragging them. Peggy will work through them in order but may adapt the flow based on how the conversation develops.

<Info>
  Set your most important topics to **High priority**. If a session runs long, Peggy will spend less time on lower-priority moments to ensure high-priority ones get covered.
</Info>

### Stimulus

You can attach stimulus materials to any moment. When Peggy reaches that moment, the stimulus is shown to all participants automatically.

Supported formats include images (PNG, JPG, GIF), videos, and URLs. Upload files directly in the moment editor.

## Screener

The screener filters participants before they join your study. Only participants who pass the screener become eligible for sessions.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The screener builder showing different question types and disqualification options]
</Frame>

### Question types

- **Multiple choice** -- Single or multi-select options
- **Open text** -- Free-form text responses
- **Number** -- Numeric input with optional range validation
- **Yes/No** -- Simple binary questions

### Disqualification

For any question, you can mark specific answers as disqualifying. Participants who select a disqualifying answer are screened out and will not be able to join sessions.

<Tip>
  Add an attention-check question to your screener (e.g., "Select the third option") to filter out participants who are not reading carefully.
</Tip>

---

## Interview settings

Configure how your sessions run:

- **Duration** -- How long each session should last (e.g., 30, 45, or 60 minutes)
- **Group size** -- Number of participants per session. Convo supports multi-participant focus groups.
- **Welcome screen** -- Customize the message participants see before the session begins

---

## Study lifecycle

Every study moves through four stages:

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Draft** | Study is being designed. You can edit everything freely. |
| **Active** | Study is live. Participants can join and sessions can run. |
| **Completed** | All sessions are done. Results are available. |
| **Archived** | Study is stored for reference but no longer active. |

<Warning>
  Once a study is Active, some settings (like the screener) cannot be changed without affecting data consistency. Make sure your screener is finalized before launching.
</Warning>
