Every study has three parts: a **discussion guide** (topics Peggy will cover), a **screener** (to qualify participants), and **interview settings** (duration, group size, welcome screen). You can build these with the AI Study Builder or the manual editor.

## AI Study Builder

The fastest way to create a study. Describe your research goals in plain language and Convo generates everything for you.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The AI Study Builder chat interface showing the step-by-step conversation flow]
</Frame>

The builder walks you through five steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe your research">
    Tell Convo what you want to learn. You can type a description or upload an existing discussion guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the discussion guide">
    Convo generates a guide with topics, questions, and follow-up probes. Edit as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your screener">
    Convo suggests screener questions based on your goals. Add, remove, or tweak them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure settings">
    Set session duration, participants per session, and customize the welcome screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch">
    Review everything and activate. You're ready to recruit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can upload an existing discussion guide (PDF or text) and Convo will convert it into the structured format automatically.
</Tip>

## Discussion guide

The discussion guide is the script Peggy follows during sessions. It's organized into **moments**, the individual topics or questions you want to cover.

### Moments

Each moment has:

- **Topic** — what Peggy will discuss
- **Goal** — what you want to learn from this part of the conversation
- **Priority** — Low, Medium, or High. High-priority moments get more time if sessions run long.
- **Probes** — follow-up questions Peggy can use to dig deeper
- **Stimulus** — optional images, videos, or other media to show participants

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: A single moment configuration showing topic, goal, priority, probes, and stimulus fields]
</Frame>

You can reorder moments by dragging them. Peggy works through them in order but may adjust based on how the conversation goes.

<Info>
  Mark your most important topics as **High priority**. If a session runs long, Peggy spends less time on lower-priority moments so the important ones get covered.
</Info>

### Stimulus

Attach media to any moment. When Peggy reaches that moment, the stimulus shows up on every participant's screen automatically.

Supported formats: images (PNG, JPG, GIF), videos, and URLs.

## Screener

The screener filters participants before they join. Only people who pass become eligible for sessions.

<Frame>
  [IMAGE: The screener builder showing different question types and disqualification options]
</Frame>

### Question types

- **Multiple choice** — single or multi-select
- **Open text** — free-form responses
- **Number** — numeric input with optional range
- **Yes/No** — binary

### Disqualification

You can mark specific answers as disqualifying. Anyone who picks a disqualifying answer gets screened out and can't join sessions.

<Tip>
  Add an attention-check question (e.g., "Select the third option") to catch people who aren't reading carefully.
</Tip>

---

## Interview settings

- **Duration** — how long each session lasts (30, 45, or 60 minutes)
- **Group size** — participants per session
- **Welcome screen** — the message participants see before the session starts

---

## Study lifecycle

Every study goes through four stages:

| Status | What it means |
|--------|-------------|
| **Draft** | Still being designed. Edit anything. |
| **Active** | Live. Participants can join, sessions can run. |
| **Completed** | Sessions are done. Results available. |
| **Archived** | Stored for reference, no longer active. |

<Warning>
  Once a study is Active, some settings (like the screener) can't be changed without affecting data consistency. Finalize your screener before launching.
</Warning>
